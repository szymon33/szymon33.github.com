ACT

ACT (Advanced Cleaning Technolgies) is a Multi-level marketing Company also called a pyramid selling company. It was founded in 1999. They focus on products mainly for cleaning and washing. They started with selling the world's first ACTEX microfiber cleanser invented by Rudolf Nordic in 1990. The application is based on three pillars. Public pages which include products, news, promotions, inquiries, articles and more. Consultants' zone which includes orders and their progress, actvity report, promotions, monthly journals, marketing gadgets, download documents section, ad-hoc notifications and more. The last part is a CMS in the back-end, to rule them all. This is the most complicated web application I have done all alone. It is not fully finished. Technology stack: Ruby 2.1, Rails 4.2, Devise Token, S3, Rspec, Capybara, Markup, PostgreSQL, Rmagic, jQuery, JSON API (microservices) among front-end and back-end, Angular 1.3, local storage, ngResource, ng-token-auth, ur.router, Jasmine, Nginx, Unicorn and more. Take a closer look. However, you can't access consultants' zone unless you are the ACT consultant or back-end zone unless you are an employee of ACT.

Cloud and Multimedia Klett

Klett Group is one of the largest and most respected educational publishers in Europe. They have created textbooks for all types of schools since 1898. I have developed a couple of simple web services for them.
The first one is called Chmura Klett (in translation 'Cloud Klett') which supports teachers who are using Klett's textbooks. It provides teaching aids for these books. Content includes mostly audio lessons but also video (like experience nature), .PDF files (like tests), pictures and .ZIPs. All is stored on S3. It includes audio stream technique. The service is public and free but you probably will need Klett's paper book. Take a closer look.
The second app is called Multimedia Klett which is a catalogue with a search engine for all Klett's on-line resources like interactive exercises, e-books, e-shop content, youtube movies, claud (described before) and others spreaded widely through the internet. Take a closer look.

Ecowater

Ecowater is one of the world's largest manufacturers of domestic water treatment systems. They have nearly 90 years experience in implementing new technology. They provide technical support for installed devices. This is my most advanced CMS so I haven't described any other CMS in this portfolio. The application consists of three parts. Front end pages which include products, news, promotions, enquiries, articles, interesting facts about water and more. Partners' zone which includes video tutorials for service, simple shop with gadgets, download documents section and more. The last part is CMS in the back-end, to rule them all. As you can see on the first screenshot the service has responsive design. Ecowater graphic design is not mine. Many people have worked to write the site's content. Take a closer look with different language versions as well as site locations: Polish, Bulgarian, Czech, Lithuanian and Slovak
